# Project Brief: Utopia Re-Imagined (Interactive Book Report)

## 1. Project Overview
An interactive, web-based book report for Rutger Bregman’s *Utopia for Realists*. The project translates radical socio-economic ideas into a visually engaging, narrative-driven experience using a classic comic book aesthetic.

## 2. Design Vision & Aesthetic
*   **Style:** "Silver Age" Comic Book (circa 1960s).
*   **Color Palette:** High-contrast primary colors—**Action Blue**, **Caution Yellow**, and **Impact Red**—balanced with off-white newsprint backgrounds.
*   **Typography:** Bold, sans-serif display fonts (Epilogue) with heavy black weights, uppercase styling, and occasional italicized "speed" variants.
*   **Visual Language:** 
    *   Heavy black borders and "ink" outlines.
    *   Halftone dot textures and "newsprint" overlays.
    *   Comic panels, speech bubbles, and "KAPOW!" style action bursts for data points.

## 3. Core Structure (The "Topics")
The report is organized into a Splash Page and 7 thematic "Issues":

1.  **Splash Page (The Origin Story):** Introduction to the book's premise and author Rutger Bregman ("The Creator").
2.  **Topic #1: Human Progress (Ch. 1):** Establishing that the world is better than it used to be, setting the stage for even bolder progress.
3.  **Topic #2: Free Money (Ch. 2-4):** The case for Universal Basic Income as an investment, not a handout.
4.  **Topic #3: Beyond GDP (Ch. 5):** Critique of GDP as a metric and the need for direct, human-centric goals.
5.  **Topic #4: Leisure Time (Ch. 6):** The history and benefits of a shorter (15-hour) work week.
6.  **Topic #5: Useless Jobs (Ch. 7-8):** Examining "bullshit jobs" and the decoupling of work from survival.
7.  **Topic #6: Open Borders (Ch. 9):** The economic and humanitarian case for global mobility.
8.  **Topic #7: Radical Ideas (Ch. 10):** The "Overton Window" and the importance of utopian thinking in shifting political reality.

## 4. Navigation & Interface
*   **Sidebar:** A fixed, high-contrast yellow sidebar containing the "Topics List" for easy jumping between sections.
*   **Footer:** A simplified, consistent black footer with centered attribution: "Made by Om Gupta."
*   **Layout:** Desktop-first design using a structured grid that mimics the panels of a comic book page.

## 5. Key UX Elements
*   **Action Bursts:** Used to highlight shocking statistics or key takeaways.
*   **Speech Bubbles:** Used for direct quotes from the book or common myths being debunked.
*   **System Status Meters:** Visual gauges (styled like power meters) to represent economic or social metrics.
